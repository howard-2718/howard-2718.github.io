Remark 1.1.1. Problem of the Week, No. 1.

Let \(f: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) be a function differentiable on \(\mathbb{R}\text{.}\) A number \(a\) is called a fixed point of said function \(f\) if \(f(a) = a\text{.}\) Prove that if \(f'(x) \neq 1\) for all real numbers \(x\text{,}\) then \(f\)has at most one fixed point.
